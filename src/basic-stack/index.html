Below you see a basic-stack element containing a landscape image and a portrait image. The basic-stack has a border applied so you can see the element's bounds. As you resize (or rotate) the window, note that the basic-stack updates its size to be the maximium height and width of its two children. Significantly, the source does not require that the heights of those child elements be pre-calculated at design time and fixed in CSS.

Any elements below the basic-stack will remain in place regardless of which of its children are visible.
